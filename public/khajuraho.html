Khajuraho Temples

Discover the magnificent UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring exquisite sandstone temples with intricate sculptures built by the Chandela dynasty between the 9th and 13th centuries.

Ancient Heritage & Architecture

Chandela Dynasty Legacy

Built by the Chandela dynasty between 950-1050 AD, the Khajuraho temples represent the pinnacle of medieval Indian architecture. Originally 85 temples, only 25 survive today, showcasing the finest examples of Nagara-style temple architecture.

UNESCO World Heritage

Designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1986, Khajuraho is celebrated for its exceptional artistic and architectural achievements, particularly the intricate stone carvings that depict various aspects of life, spirituality, and human emotions.

Three Temple Groups

The temples are divided into three groups: Western (Hindu temples), Eastern (Jain temples), and Southern (mixed). Each group showcases different architectural styles and religious significance, reflecting the cultural diversity of medieval India.

Nearby Must-Visit Attractions

Panna National Park

Wildlife sanctuary located 25km from Khajuraho, home to tigers, leopards, and diverse bird species. Perfect for nature enthusiasts and wildlife photography.

Raneh Falls

Stunning natural waterfall on the Ken River, 20km from Khajuraho, featuring unique rock formations and crystal-clear waters surrounded by lush greenery.

Ajaygarh Fort

Ancient fort built by the Chandela rulers, offering panoramic views of the surrounding landscape and insights into the region's military history.

Beni Sagar Dam

Scenic dam and reservoir perfect for boating and picnics, offering a peaceful retreat with beautiful sunset views over the water.
