Fatehpur Sikri

Explore the magnificent Mughal capital built by Emperor Akbar, featuring stunning architecture that blends Persian, Indian, and Islamic styles in a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Mughal Heritage & History

Akbar's Dream City

Founded by Emperor Akbar in 1571, Fatehpur Sikri served as the Mughal capital for 14 years. Built to honor the Sufi saint Salim Chishti, the city represents Akbar's vision of religious tolerance and architectural innovation.

UNESCO World Heritage

Designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1986, Fatehpur Sikri showcases the finest examples of Mughal architecture, featuring red sandstone buildings with intricate carvings and innovative design elements.

Architectural Marvel

The city's architecture represents a unique fusion of Persian, Indian, and Islamic styles, featuring the world's highest gateway (Buland Darwaza), magnificent palaces, and the sacred tomb of Salim Chishti.

Historical & Cultural Activities


Palace Complex Tour

Explore the magnificent palaces including Panch Mahal, Diwan-i-Khas, and Diwan-i-Aam while learning about Mughal court life and architectural innovations.

Duration: 2–3 hours

Buland Darwaza Visit

Marvel at the world's highest gateway, built to commemorate Akbar's victory over Gujarat, and learn about its architectural significance and historical importance.

Duration: 30–45 mins

Jama Masjid Exploration

Visit one of India's largest mosques and the sacred tomb of Salim Chishti, experiencing the spiritual atmosphere and architectural grandeur.

Duration: 1–2 hours

Mughal Architecture Workshop

Learn about Mughal architectural techniques, design principles, and the cultural fusion that created this magnificent city.

Duration: 2–3 hours

Nearby Must-Visit Attractions

Taj Mahal, Agra

World-famous white marble mausoleum located 40km away, representing the pinnacle of Mughal architecture and a symbol of eternal love.

Agra Fort

UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring magnificent Mughal architecture, royal palaces, and stunning views of the Taj Mahal from its ramparts.

Keoladeo National Park

Bharatpur Bird Sanctuary, 25km from Fatehpur Sikri, home to over 370 bird species including migratory birds and diverse wildlife.

Itmad-ud-Daulah Tomb

Known as the "Baby Taj," this exquisite tomb in Agra showcases intricate marble inlay work and represents a transition in Mughal architecture.
